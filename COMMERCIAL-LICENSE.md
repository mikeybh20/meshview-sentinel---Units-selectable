## Who needs the Commercial License?

You need the Commercial License if any of the following apply to your
intended use of MeshView Sentinel:

- You intend to **integrate the Software into a proprietary product** or
  service whose source code you do not intend to release under the AGPL.
- You intend to **offer the Software (modified or unmodified) as a
  hosted service to third parties** without making the corresponding
  source code (including your modifications) available to those users
  under the AGPL.
- You intend to **redistribute the Software as part of a closed-source
  bundle** to your customers.
- You require **warranty, indemnification, or formal commercial support
  terms** that the AGPL-3.0 does not provide.
- You are a **commercial entity using the Software for revenue-generating
  purposes** and you are unable to comply with AGPL-3.0's copyleft
  obligations (including the §13 "remote network interaction" clause,
  which extends copyleft to network-accessible deployments).

You do **not** need the Commercial License if you fall into one of the
categories enumerated in the [Free Commercial License Policy](FREE-USE-POLICY.md).
Those categories receive a free Commercial License waiver on request.

## What does the Commercial License grant?

The Commercial License grants the licensee:

- **Exemption from AGPL-3.0 copyleft obligations** — the licensee may
  use, modify, deploy, and integrate the Software without the
  source-disclosure requirements of AGPL-3.0 §5 (corresponding source)
  and §13 (remote network interaction).
- **The right to incorporate the Software into proprietary derivative
  works** without those derivative works being subject to AGPL-3.0.
- **Limited warranty and indemnification** as specified in the
  individual commercial license agreement.
- **Commercial support** terms as agreed in the individual license
  agreement.

The Commercial License does **not** grant:

- Ownership of the underlying copyright in the Software.
- The right to remove attribution to the original authors.
- The right to relicense the Software to third parties.
- The right to claim authorship of the Software.

## How is the Commercial License priced?

Commercial license fees are negotiated per-engagement and depend on:

- Deployment scale (number of nodes, number of users, transaction
  volume).
- Whether modifications will remain private or be contributed back.
- Required support level (none, standard, priority).
- Required warranty and indemnification scope.

Royalty revenue from Commercial License sales is split between the
co-authors per a private co-author agreement and is not relevant to the
licensee.

## Why a dual-license model?

MeshView Sentinel exists to serve mesh-network operators, amateur radio
communities, and emergency communications volunteers — for whom the
Software is free under AGPL-3.0 in perpetuity. The Commercial License
exists so that commercial entities deriving revenue from the Software
contribute back to the project (either through source-code contribution
under AGPL, or through licensing revenue) rather than free-riding on
volunteer effort.

If you are unsure whether you need the Commercial License, please reach
out — the conversation costs nothing and avoids surprises later.
