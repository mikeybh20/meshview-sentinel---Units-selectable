## Categories Eligible for Free Commercial License Waiver

A request from any of the following will be granted at $0 unless the
copyright holders identify specific concerns (which would be disclosed
in the response):

### 1. Individual use
Individuals using the Software for personal, household, or hobbyist
purposes. No request required for AGPL-only use; request a waiver only
if you need the Commercial License's exemptions for some specific
reason (e.g., distributing a modified version privately to family
members without offering source).

### 2. Amateur radio organizations
- **ARRL-affiliated clubs** and **RAC-affiliated clubs** (Canada).
- Local amateur radio clubs operating non-commercially.
- Mesh-network community groups (e.g., DC Mesh, Frederick area mesh,
  and equivalent regional groups).
- Operators of free-to-use repeaters, mesh nodes, or relay
  infrastructure.

### 3. Volunteer emergency communications
- **SKYWARN** spotter networks.
- **ARES** (Amateur Radio Emergency Service).
- **RACES** (Radio Amateur Civil Emergency Service).
- **MARS** (Military Auxiliary Radio System) — volunteer affiliates.
- **CERT** (Community Emergency Response Team) chapters.
- **REACT** (Radio Emergency Associated Communication Teams).
- Other registered volunteer emergency-communications organizations.

### 4. Volunteer first responders
- Volunteer fire departments.
- Volunteer rescue squads.
- Volunteer search-and-rescue (SAR) teams.
- Volunteer ambulance services.
- Mountain rescue / wilderness rescue volunteer organizations.

### 5. Registered non-profits with EmComm or community mission
- **501(c)(3) organizations** in the United States whose mission
  includes emergency communications, disaster response, public-safety
  communications, or community mesh networking.
- Equivalent registered non-profits in other jurisdictions.

### 6. Educational use
- Academic research projects at non-commercial educational
  institutions.
- Student projects, capstone projects, theses.
- Classroom instruction and demonstration use.

## Categories NOT Eligible for Free Commercial License Waiver

The following categories must purchase a Commercial License at the
negotiated commercial rate:

### Commercial / revenue-generating use
- For-profit businesses deploying MeshView Sentinel in production for
  any revenue-related purpose, including but not limited to:
  - Internal operational use that supports a revenue-generating
    business line.
  - Hosting the Software as a paid service for third parties.
  - Bundling the Software with a commercial product.
  - Using the Software to deliver paid consulting services.

### Law enforcement
- All law enforcement agencies (local, state, federal, tribal,
  international) at all levels — regardless of operational use case.
  Law enforcement deployments require a Commercial License with
  specific terms negotiated per-deployment.

### Government contractors
- Government contractors providing services to law enforcement,
  intelligence, defense, or surveillance agencies. Civil-defense and
  emergency-management contracts are evaluated case-by-case.

### Resellers and integrators
- Parties intending to resell, redistribute, or integrate the Software
  into a commercial offering (whether as a SaaS, an on-premise product,
  or an embedded component).

## How to request a Free Commercial License Waiver

Email **michael.broadwater@gmail.com** with the subject line
"MeshView Sentinel Free Commercial License Waiver Request" and include:

- The name of the requesting entity.
- The eligibility category from this document (e.g., "ARES — Section
  3").
- Evidence of eligibility where appropriate (a link to the
  organization's public roster, ARRL affiliation page, IRS 501(c)(3)
  determination letter, etc.).
- A brief description of the intended use.
- The approximate deployment scale (number of nodes, number of users).

Responses typically arrive within 30 days. The waiver, when granted,
takes the form of an executed Commercial License agreement at $0 fee
that the eligible entity countersigns.

## Edge cases

If you're unsure whether you qualify:

- **You operate a mesh node that occasionally relays paid traffic** —
  qualifies as long as the node itself is not a revenue-generating
  asset. Free.
- **You're a paid SKYWARN coordinator employed by a county
  emergency-management office** — the employment context is
  commercial-by-classification, but the SKYWARN mission is volunteer
  EmComm. Reach out; this is usually granted as a free waiver with
  documentation noting the dual character.
- **You're a HAM radio operator using the Software at your workplace
  during your lunch break** — the personal use is fine under AGPL;
  no Commercial License needed.
- **You're a fire department considering Sentinel for active
  operations** — volunteer fire department is Section 4, free.
  Paid municipal fire department is evaluated under the
  "government contractors" question; reach out.
- **You're a university researcher writing a paper that uses the
  Software** — Section 6, free.

When in doubt, send the request. The conversation costs nothing and
prevents misunderstandings later.
